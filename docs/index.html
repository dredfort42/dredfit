How it works

A thermostat, not a questionnaire

Other apps ask you twenty questions and hope. Dredfit asks one — after the workout — and knows.

1

Start at a comfortable minimum

Day one is deliberately easy — every movement starts at the bottom of its range. From there the regulator raises the load after each session until it settles at the edge of what you can actually do. No fitness test required.

2

One integer per movement

Your progress is one small number per movement — ten of them. Each level encodes the variation, the rep target and the set count at once: harder variations unlock automatically, and above the top tier the sets grow instead.

tier = min(4, 1 + L / 8) · reps = 8 + L % 8
3

Answer one question

Tough, on plan, or easy — one tap and the next workout adjusts. When a single movement falls short three times running, that movement alone rolls back a few steps and starts again. Progress without burnout.

What's inside

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

40 exercises, household propsA chair, a table, a wall, a towel and a step cover 36 of them — knee push-ups to archer push-ups, squats to shrimp squats. The four pull-up variations are the only ones that need a bar, and they stay off until you switch them on.
Balanced by designA pull in every session for shoulder health; the other patterns rotate so each comes round five times in eight workouts.
Got a pull-up bar?Turn it on and every other session swaps the floor pull for a vertical one — bar hang through full pull-ups, tracked separately.
Adjust in the momentDid fewer reps? Dial the real number right at the exercise, without leaving it. Skipping is free — a skipped exercise simply doesn't count.
Warm-up, holds and rest, timedA guided warm-up, a hold timer for planks, and a rest ring with a 3-2-1 countdown. Every timer is wall-clock based, so locking the phone loses nothing.
Progress you can seeA level chart across sessions, a weekly summary, per-movement bars, and a calendar of everything you've done.
On your lock screenA Live Activity counts the rest down on the lock screen and Dynamic Island; a home-screen widget shows today at a glance.
Fits your weekPick your rest days, get a reminder on training days, and export or import your whole history as plain JSON.
Plain-language techniqueThree steps and two common mistakes for every exercise, in English and Russian. No jargon.
0

Zero data collected

No analytics, no ads, no third-party SDKs, no network requests at all. Dredfit has no servers and never transmits your training history — it is stored on your device and never reaches us. That's not a promise, it's the architecture.

It leaves your phone only when you decide: a JSON backup you export and share yourself, workouts you opt into writing to Apple Health, or a standard iOS device backup. The privacy policy spells all three out.
