Privacy Policy

Nothing to collect.
Nothing to hide.

Effective date: July 19, 2026
0

Dredfit does not collect any data. None. No account system, no analytics, no advertising, no third-party SDKs, and no network requests of any kind.

Where your data lives

Your entire training history — workout records, levels, and adjustments — is stored locally on your device. Dredfit has no servers and never transmits it. If you delete the app, this data is deleted with it — the one exception being workouts already written to Apple Health, which stay in the Health app until you remove them there.

Standard iOS device backups (iCloud or local) may include the app's data as part of your device backup, under Apple's own privacy terms.

You can export a complete backup to a plain JSON file at any time from Settings. The backup is the app's whole state — your training history together with your settings (among them rest days, reminder time, sounds, the pull-up-bar switch, and the Health-export bookkeeping). That file goes wherever you send it through the iOS share sheet — we never see it. You can also import such a backup back into the app: importing only reads the file you picked and transmits nothing.

After a milestone — and from the Progress tab — Dredfit offers a share card: a small image with your workout count and total level (or the milestone itself) and the date, rendered entirely on your device. Like the backup, it goes only where you send it through the share sheet, and nowhere otherwise.

Apple Health

Dredfit can write finished workouts to Apple Health. This is off by default and only starts after you enable it in Settings and grant permission.

When it is on, each completed workout is written as a single Functional Strength Training entry containing its start time, end time and duration, plus the standard device record iOS attaches to any Health entry (device model and OS version). No heart rate, no calories, no body measurements, no exercise details.

The permission is write-only: Dredfit asks for no read access at all and cannot see anything already in your Health data. Health data stays on your device under Apple's protection, and we never receive it.

You can revoke access any time in Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Dredfit, or turn the switch off inside the app. Workouts already written stay in Health until you delete them there.

Notifications

If you enable the reminder, Dredfit schedules a local notification on your training days. It is generated and delivered on your device — there is no push server and nothing is transmitted.

Third parties

We never receive your data, so there is nothing for us to share, sell, or process, and nothing is processed on any server. Dredfit contains no third-party SDKs, no analytics and no advertising, and makes no network requests of any kind.

The only party besides you your data can ever reach is Apple, and only through operating-system features you control: workouts you opt into writing to Apple Health, and app data included in a standard iCloud or local device backup. In both cases it is held under Apple's privacy terms, and we never see it.

Retention and your rights

We retain nothing, so there is no retention period on our side: your data lives on your device until you delete the app, and we never hold a copy. Privacy laws such as the GDPR and CCPA give you rights of access, deletion, and portability — with Dredfit you exercise all of them directly on the device: the JSON export is your complete portable copy of everything the app stores, and deleting the app is erasure — with the one exception noted above: workouts already written to Apple Health stay there until you remove them in the Health app. There is nothing to request from us, because there is nothing we could produce; questions are still welcome at the address below.

Children

Dredfit is a general-fitness tool for a general audience and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children — because it does not collect data at all.

Changes to this policy
